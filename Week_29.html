Section 1. Weekly Grammar Point (5 mins)

Inversion
• Structure: Negative Adverb + Aux Verb + Subject + Verb.
• Use: For emphasis/formal tone.
• Rarely do we see such skill. (Normal: We rarely see...)
• Never have I been so tired.
倒装句：否定副词 + 助动词 + 主语 + 动词。用于强调语气。

Section 2. Weekly Pronunciation Point (3 mins)

Emphasis in Inversion
Stress the negative adverb at the start.
• NEVER have I seen that!
• RARELY do we go there.
倒装句的重音：重读句首的否定副词。

Recycled Words: (Included in list above)
Idiom 1: Throw in the towel (认输 / 放弃)
Explanation: 承认失败，放弃努力 (源自拳击比赛)。
Example: Learning to play the violin is hard, but you shouldn't throw in the towel yet.
Idiom 2: Give it one's best shot (全力以赴)
Explanation: 竭尽全力去尝试做某事。
Example: I am not great at painting, but I will give it my best shot.

Section 4. Warm-Up Questions (10 mins)

  1. (Section 5 Question) Is the high prestige of professional athletes justified?
  2. Are sports indispensable for a society to flourish? (Recycled)
  3. Do you prefer amateur or professional sports?

Section 5. Shadowing text adapted from the Cambridge IGCSE ESL Coursebook (3 mins)

It is shocking that athletes earn so much. Rarely do we see such high salaries in other jobs. Actually, brands pay them millions to endorse products. Never have I seen an amateur player gain such prestige, even if they work harder. However, athletes must persevere through pain. They exert enormous energy. Little do people realize that team solidarity is indispensable for success. If sports are to flourish, we need fairness. Don't throw in the towel just because it's hard. Always give it your best shot.

Section 7. Model Answer (5 mins)

It is often debated whether athletes are overpaid. Actually, their careers are short. To be honest, rarely do people work as physically hard as they do. Not only do they exert themselves daily, but they also inspire solidarity among fans. However, teachers are also indispensable but earn less. Seldom have I seen a teacher flourish financially like a sports star. Brands endorse athletes for their prestige. Even an amateur must persevere. To sum up, the market decides.

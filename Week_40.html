Section 1. Weekly Grammar Point (5 mins)

Bound to / Due to / On the verge of
• Bound to: Certain to happen. It is bound to rain.
• Due to: Because of. Due to the storm, we stopped.
• On the verge of: Very close to happening. We are on the verge of a breakthrough.
高级预测：Bound to (注定), Due to (由于), On the verge of (濒临/即将)。

Recycled Words: (Included in list above)
Idiom 1: Only time will tell (时间会证明一切)
Explanation: 只有时间才能证明或揭晓结果。
Example: Will AI take over all human jobs? Only time will tell.
Idiom 2: Cross that bridge when we come to it (船到桥头自然直)
Explanation: 船到桥头自然直，到时候再解决问题。
Example: Don't worry about the final exams yet; we will cross that bridge when we come to it.

Section 5. Shadowing text adapted from the Cambridge IGCSE ESL Coursebook (3 mins)

It is exciting to speculate about 2070. However, a climate crisis looms over us. Actually, we face a serious dilemma. Due to pollution, the environment is bound to deteriorate further unless we act. Scientists anticipate major changes. We are on the verge of a breakthrough that could restore the optimum balance of nature. We need visionary leaders. Otherwise, suffering will become prevalent. Will we succeed? Only time will tell. As for aliens, we will cross that bridge when we come to it.

Section 7. Model Answer (5 mins)

It is hard to know what lies ahead. Actually, a resource war looms. To be honest, this is our biggest dilemma. Due to overpopulation, shortages are bound to become prevalent. However, I anticipate that visionary inventors will save us. We are on the verge of finding the optimum energy source. If not, society will deteriorate. Can we speculate on peace? Only time will tell. For now, we tackle today's problems and cross that bridge when we come to it.

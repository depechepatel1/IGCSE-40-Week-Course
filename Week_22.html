Section 1. Weekly Grammar Point (5 mins)

Complex Comparisons
• Modifiers: Far more, slightly less, nowhere near as... as.
• This report is far more biased than that one.
• TV is nowhere near as popular as it used to be.
复杂比较级：使用修饰语 (far more, slightly less) 来加强或减弱比较的程度。

Section 2. Weekly Pronunciation Point (3 mins)

Stress in Complex Comparisons
Stress the modifier for emphasis.
• It is FAR more interesting.
• It is NOwhere near as good.
比较级的重音：重读修饰语以加强语气。

Section 5. Shadowing text adapted from the Cambridge IGCSE ESL Coursebook (3 mins)

It is undeniable that media influences us. Unfortunately, some news outlets manipulate facts to gain views. Actually, they often sensationalize stories. It is far more difficult to find objective reporting today than it used to be. However, we must try to verify what we read. Personally, I take with a grain of salt everything I see online. Social media disseminates information much faster than traditional TV, but it often contains more bias. Some content is designed to stimulate anger rather than inform. Therefore, critical thinking is essential.

Section 7. Model Answer (5 mins)

It is often observed that media companies pursue profits over truth. Consequently, they may manipulate headlines to attract clicks. To be honest, this practice sensationalizes trivial events. This report is nowhere near as objective as it claims to be, showing clear political bias. Moreover, fake news is easy to disseminate via word of mouth online. It is slightly less difficult to verify facts if you check multiple sources. We should stimulate open discussions instead of echoing one view. To sum up, skepticism is a necessary tool.

Section 1. Weekly Grammar Point (5 mins)

Too / Enough
• Too + Adjective: More than necessary (Negative). (e.g., It is too expensive).
• Adjective + Enough: Sufficient (Positive). (e.g., It is fast enough).
Too / Enough：Too (太...) 表示否定/过多；Enough (足够...) 表示肯定/适量，放在形容词后。

Section 2. Weekly Pronunciation Point (3 mins)

Hard vs. Soft 'th' sounds
• Soft /θ/: Think, Technology, Breakthrough (No vibration).
• Hard /ð/: The, This, That (Vibration).
Th 的发音：清辅音 /θ/ (think)，浊辅音 /ð/ (this)。

Idiom 1: Not rocket science (并非难事 / 显而易见)
Explanation: 形容某事并不复杂，很容易理解。
Example: You can easily fix the computer problem; it's not rocket science.
Idiom 2: Ahead of the curve (走在时代前列 / 遥遥领先)
Explanation: 在思想或技术上领先于时代或其他人。
Example: That tech company is always ahead of the curve with its smartphone designs.

Section 5. Shadowing text adapted from the Cambridge IGCSE ESL Coursebook (3 mins)

To be honest, technology changes very fast. My old phone is completely outdated now. Actually, it is too slow to run new apps. I want to buy an innovative new gadget, but I don't have enough money. However, there has been a major breakthrough in battery life recently. So, I plan to upgrade soon. Also, modern phones are more eco-friendly and sustainable. Using a smartphone is not rocket science, so anyone can learn it quickly. In my opinion, virtual assistants are very helpful.

Section 7. Model Answer (5 mins)

Well, I use my tablet every day. Actually, it is an innovative device. To be honest, my laptop was too heavy to carry, but this tablet is light enough. It allows me to enter a virtual classroom and study online, which is very sustainable. Also, I often use it to control other smart gadgets in my home. Because the company is ahead of the curve, they release a major upgrade every year. Although my old tablet is outdated, it is still eco-friendly to recycle it. To sum up, it is a major technological breakthrough for students.

Section 1. Weekly Grammar Point (5 mins)

Mixed Conditionals
• Past Action -> Present Result: If + Past Perfect, ... would + Verb.
• If I had studied (past), I would be rich (now).
混合条件句：If + 过去完成时 (过去的动作), ... would + 动词原形 (现在的后果)。

Section 2. Weekly Pronunciation Point (3 mins)

Sentence Rhythm
Keep a steady rhythm. Stress content words (nouns, verbs) and glide over function words.
• If I had known, I would have gone.
句子节奏：保持稳定的节奏。重读实词 (名词、动词)，弱读虚词。

Idiom 1: Put one's thinking cap on (绞尽脑汁 / 认真思考)
Explanation: 准备认真思考，绞尽脑汁解决问题。
Example: We need to put our thinking caps on to figure out this difficult math problem.
Idiom 2: Learn the hard way (吃亏长智 / 花钱买教训)
Explanation: 通过遭受惨痛的教训才学到经验。
Example: I didn't save any money in my twenties, so I had to learn the hard way when I lost my job.

Section 5. Shadowing text adapted from the Cambridge IGCSE ESL Coursebook (3 mins)

It is commonly believed that education is the key to success. However, some systems focus too much on theoretical knowledge. Actually, students often have to memorize facts without understanding them. If schools had focused on cognitive skills earlier, students would be better problem-solvers today. We need a more comprehensive approach. For example, we should cultivate creativity, not just memory. If I hadn't had the chance to immerse myself in authentic projects, I wouldn't be so confident now. Unfortunately, many people learn the hard way that grades aren't everything. We must put our thinking caps on to improve the system.

Section 7. Model Answer (5 mins)

It is often debated whether schools should teach facts or skills. Actually, both are important. To be honest, pure theoretical learning can be boring. If I hadn't been forced to memorize so many dates, I would enjoy history more now. However, we need to evaluate evidence critically. If schools had cultivated more cognitive abilities in the past, society would be smarter today. I believe we need authentic tasks that force us to immerse in real work. To sum up, a comprehensive education balances both.

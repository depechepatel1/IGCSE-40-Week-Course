📌 PART B: The 40-Week Spiral Curriculum Map (Lucantoni Adaptation)
Note to Gemini: Weeks 1–20 focus on concrete IELTS Part 1 & 2 topics (Foundation). Weeks 21–40 revisit the exact same Lucantoni 20 themes but focus on IELTS Part 3 abstract logic, societal impacts, and advanced grammar.

📦 BATCH 1: Foundation Phase (Weeks 1–5)
Focus: Basic habits, descriptions, and past events.

Week 1 (Unit 1: Leisure/Free Time):

Grammar: Adverbs of Frequency & Gerunds

Pronunciation: Connected Speech (Consonant to Vowel)

Prompt Focus: Describe a regular hobby.

Week 2 (Unit 2: TV & Media):

Grammar: Comparatives & Superlatives

Pronunciation: Word Stress in multi-syllable adjectives

Prompt Focus: Describe a memorable movie/show.

Week 3 (Unit 3: Food & Diet):

Grammar: Quantifiers (too much/many) & Present Perfect

Pronunciation: Long Vowel Sounds

Prompt Focus: Describe a memorable meal.

Week 4 (Unit 4: Transport):

Grammar: First Conditional (If + Present, Will)

Pronunciation: Sentence stress in conditionals

Prompt Focus: Describe a long journey.

Week 5 (Unit 5: Holidays):

Grammar: Past Continuous vs. Past Simple

Pronunciation: Weak forms of "was/were"

Prompt Focus: Describe an unforgettable trip.

📦 BATCH 2: Foundation Phase (Weeks 6–10)
Focus: Experiences, obligations, and routines.

Week 6 (Unit 6: Learning/Education):

Grammar: Used to & Would (Past habits)

Pronunciation: Enunciation of "-ed" endings

Prompt Focus: Describe a favorite school subject.

Week 7 (Unit 7: Careers):

Grammar: Modal Verbs of Obligation (must, have to, should)

Pronunciation: Silent letters (e.g., colleague, resign)

Prompt Focus: Describe a job you would like to have.

Week 8 (Unit 8: Work & Money):

Grammar: Second Conditional (If + Past, Would)

Pronunciation: Contractions (I'd, wouldn't)

Prompt Focus: Describe something expensive you saved up for.

Week 9 (Unit 9: Sports):

Grammar: Present Perfect Continuous (have been V-ing)

Pronunciation: Rhythm and pacing in lists

Prompt Focus: Describe a sports match you watched or played.

Week 10 (Unit 10: People/Relationships):

Grammar: Defining Relative Clauses (who, which, that)

Pronunciation: Pausing at relative pronouns

Prompt Focus: Describe a friend who is important to you.

📦 BATCH 3: Foundation Phase (Weeks 11–15)
Focus: Passive structures, continuous actions, and hypothetical situations.

Week 11 (Unit 11: Organizations/Volunteering):

Grammar: Passive Voice (Present & Past)

Pronunciation: Stress on the main verb in passive structures

Prompt Focus: Describe a time you helped someone.

Week 12 (Unit 12: Achievements):

Grammar: Past Perfect (had + V3)

Pronunciation: Dropping the 'h' in rapid speech ("I'd")

Prompt Focus: Describe a difficult task you succeeded in.

Week 13 (Unit 13: Environment):

Grammar: Future Intentions (going to vs. will)

Pronunciation: Schwa sound in "going to" (gonna)

Prompt Focus: Describe a beautiful natural place.

Week 14 (Unit 14: Technology):

Grammar: Too / Enough + Adjective

Pronunciation: Hard vs. Soft 'th' sounds

Prompt Focus: Describe a piece of technology you use daily.

Week 15 (Unit 15: Culture/Customs):

Grammar: Question Tags (isn't it? do they?)

Pronunciation: Rising vs. Falling intonation for question tags

Prompt Focus: Describe a traditional festival.

📦 BATCH 4: Foundation Phase (Weeks 16–20)
Focus: Advanced basics and future predictions.

Week 16 (Unit 16: Modern Living/City vs Country):

Grammar: As... As (Comparisons of equality)

Pronunciation: Linking 's' sounds

Prompt Focus: Describe the city or town where you live.

Week 17 (Unit 17: Fashion/Shopping):

Grammar: Causative Verbs (have/get something done)

Pronunciation: Syllable counting

Prompt Focus: Describe a favorite item of clothing.

Week 18 (Unit 18: Entertainment/Arts):

Grammar: Adjective Order

Pronunciation: Expressive intonation for opinions

Prompt Focus: Describe an art exhibition or performance.

Week 19 (Unit 19: Health/Medicine):

Grammar: Reported Speech

Pronunciation: Vowel shifting in reported speech

Prompt Focus: Describe a time you were sick and recovered.

Week 20 (Unit 20: The Future):

Grammar: Future Continuous (will be V-ing)

Pronunciation: Stressing the '-ing' suffix

Prompt Focus: Describe your life 10 years from now.

📦 BATCH 5: Advanced Phase (Weeks 21–25)
Focus: Revisiting Units 1-5 for IELTS Part 3 (Abstract Logic & Societal Impacts).

Week 21 (Unit 1: Leisure): Grammar: Hedging (It could be argued...). | Prompt: How has technology changed societal relaxation?

Week 22 (Unit 2: TV & Media): Grammar: Complex Comparisons. | Prompt: What are the dangers of media bias and manipulation?

Week 23 (Unit 3: Food & Diet): Grammar: Third Conditional. | Prompt: Why is there a global epidemic of unhealthy eating?

Week 24 (Unit 4: Transport): Grammar: Future Perfect. | Prompt: How can cities solve gridlock sustainably?

Week 25 (Unit 5: Holidays): Grammar: Non-defining Relative Clauses. | Prompt: What is the cultural impact of commercializing pristine destinations?

📦 BATCH 6: Advanced Phase (Weeks 26–30)
Focus: Revisiting Units 6-10 for IELTS Part 3.

Week 26 (Unit 6: Education): Grammar: Mixed Conditionals. | Prompt: Should schools prioritize theoretical learning or practical skills?

Week 27 (Unit 7: Careers): Grammar: Passive Reporting Verbs (It is believed...). | Prompt: How is the corporate hierarchy changing?

Week 28 (Unit 8: Work & Money): Grammar: Participle Clauses (Having worked hard...). | Prompt: What are the consequences of a materialistic society?

Week 29 (Unit 9: Sports): Grammar: Inversion (Rarely do we...). | Prompt: Is the high salary of professional athletes justified?

Week 30 (Unit 10: Relationships): Grammar: Cleft Sentences (What I find most interesting is...). | Prompt: How has digital communication affected human empathy?

📦 BATCH 7: Advanced Phase (Weeks 31–35)
Focus: Revisiting Units 11-15 for IELTS Part 3.

Week 31 (Unit 11: Volunteering): Grammar: Subjunctive / Wishes. | Prompt: Do corporations have a moral duty to engage in philanthropy?

Week 32 (Unit 12: Achievements): Grammar: Advanced Gerunds. | Prompt: What role do resilience and tenacity play in overcoming obstacles?

Week 33 (Unit 13: Environment): Grammar: Double Comparatives (The more... the more...). | Prompt: Is irreversible ecological damage inevitable?

Week 34 (Unit 14: Technology): Grammar: Future in the Past (was going to). | Prompt: How will workplace automation force society to adapt?

Week 35 (Unit 15: Culture): Grammar: Ellipsis & Substitution. | Prompt: Will globalization inevitably homogenize diverse cultures?

📦 BATCH 8: Advanced Phase (Weeks 36–40)
Focus: Revisiting Units 16-20 for IELTS Part 3 (High-level debate & speculation).

Week 36 (Unit 16: Modern Living): Grammar: Concessive Clauses (Much as I like...). | Prompt: Does rapid urbanization create better amenities or social alienation?

Week 37 (Unit 17: Fashion): Grammar: 'Whatever/Whenever' Clauses. | Prompt: How does consumerism affect our perception of status?

Week 38 (Unit 18: Entertainment): Grammar: Noun Phrases as Subjects. | Prompt: Why is contemporary abstract art often controversial?

Week 39 (Unit 19: Health): Grammar: So / Such... that. | Prompt: As longevity increases, how must society adapt to holistic care?

Week 40 (Unit 20: The Future): Grammar: Bound to / Due to / On the verge of. | Prompt: Speculating on the next 50 years, what is humanity's greatest dilemma?

<h4 style="margin: 0 0 10px 0; color: #2c3e50;">Section 1. Weekly Grammar Point</h4>
<p><strong>Adverbs of Frequency & Gerunds</strong>



• <strong>Adverbs:</strong> Place them <em>before</em> the main verb but <em>after</em> the 'be' verb (e.g., <em>I usually play... / I am always happy...</em>).



• <strong>Gerunds:</strong> Verbs expressing preference (enjoy, prefer, love) are followed by a V-ing gerund (e.g., <em>I enjoy playing badminton...</em>).</p>
</div>

<div style="border: 1px solid #ccc; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 15px; border-radius: 8px; background: white; border-left: 6px solid #f39c12; box-shadow: 2px 2px 5px rgba(0,0,0,0.05);">
<h4 style="margin: 0 0 10px 0; color: #2c3e50;">Section 2. Weekly Pronunciation Point</h4>
<p><strong>Connected Speech (Linking Consonant to Vowel)</strong>



To sound like a native speaker, do not pause between words if one ends in a consonant sound and the next begins with a vowel sound. Slide them together!



• Say: <strong>have‿a blast</strong> (Sounds like: <em>ha - va - blast</em>)



• Say: <strong>couch‿potato</strong> (Smoothly connect the <em>ch</em> into the <em>p</em>)</p>
</div>

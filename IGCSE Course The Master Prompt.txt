📌 PART C: The 40-Week Spiral Curriculum Map (Lucantoni Adaptation)
Note to Gemini: Weeks 1–20 focus on concrete IELTS Part 1 & 2 topics (Foundation). Weeks 21–40 revisit the exact same Lucantoni 20 themes but focus on IELTS Part 3 abstract logic, societal impacts, and advanced grammar.

📦 BATCH 1: Foundation Phase (Weeks 1–5)
Focus: Basic habits, descriptions, and past events. (1 Complex Sentence per text)

Week 1 (Unit 1: Leisure/Free Time): Grammar: Adverbs of Frequency & Gerunds | Pronunciation: Connected Speech (Consonant to Vowel) | Prompt: Describe a regular hobby.

Week 2 (Unit 2: TV & Media): Grammar: Comparatives & Superlatives | Pronunciation: Word Stress in multi-syllable adjectives | Prompt: Describe a memorable movie/show.

Week 3 (Unit 3: Food & Diet): Grammar: Quantifiers (too much/many) & Present Perfect | Pronunciation: Long Vowel Sounds | Prompt: Describe a memorable meal.

Week 4 (Unit 4: Transport): Grammar: First Conditional (If + Present, Will) | Pronunciation: Sentence stress in conditionals | Prompt: Describe a long journey.

Week 5 (Unit 5: Holidays): Grammar: Past Continuous vs. Past Simple | Pronunciation: Weak forms of "was/were" | Prompt: Describe an unforgettable trip.

📦 BATCH 2: Foundation Phase (Weeks 6–10)
Focus: Experiences, obligations, and routines. (2 Complex Sentences per text BEGINS)

Week 6 (Unit 6: Learning/Education): Grammar: Used to & Would (Past habits) | Pronunciation: Enunciation of "-ed" endings | Prompt: Describe a favorite school subject.

Week 7 (Unit 7: Careers): Grammar: Modal Verbs of Obligation (must, have to, should) | Pronunciation: Silent letters (e.g., colleague, resign) | Prompt: Describe a job you would like to have.

Week 8 (Unit 8: Work & Money): Grammar: Second Conditional (If + Past, Would) | Pronunciation: Contractions (I'd, wouldn't) | Prompt: Describe something expensive you saved up for.

Week 9 (Unit 9: Sports): Grammar: Present Perfect Continuous (have been V-ing) | Pronunciation: Rhythm and pacing in lists | Prompt: Describe a sports match you watched or played.

Week 10 (Unit 10: People/Relationships): Grammar: Defining Relative Clauses (who, which, that) | Pronunciation: Pausing at relative pronouns | Prompt: Describe a friend who is important to you.

📦 BATCH 3: Foundation Phase (Weeks 11–15)
Focus: Passive structures, continuous actions, and hypothetical situations. (2 Complex Sentences per text)

Week 11 (Unit 11: Organizations/Volunteering): Grammar: Passive Voice (Present & Past) | Pronunciation: Stress on the main verb in passive structures | Prompt: Describe a time you helped someone.

Week 12 (Unit 12: Achievements): Grammar: Past Perfect (had + V3) | Pronunciation: Dropping the 'h' in rapid speech ("I'd") | Prompt: Describe a difficult task you succeeded in.

Week 13 (Unit 13: Environment): Grammar: Future Intentions (going to vs. will) | Pronunciation: Schwa sound in "going to" (gonna) | Prompt: Describe a beautiful natural place.

Week 14 (Unit 14: Technology): Grammar: Too / Enough + Adjective | Pronunciation: Hard vs. Soft 'th' sounds | Prompt: Describe a piece of technology you use daily.

Week 15 (Unit 15: Culture/Customs): Grammar: Question Tags (isn't it? do they?) | Pronunciation: Rising vs. Falling intonation for question tags | Prompt: Describe a traditional festival.

📦 BATCH 4: Foundation Phase (Weeks 16–20)
Focus: Advanced basics and future predictions. (2 Complex Sentences per text)

Week 16 (Unit 16: Modern Living/City vs Country): Grammar: As... As (Comparisons of equality) | Pronunciation: Linking 's' sounds | Prompt: Describe the city or town where you live.

Week 17 (Unit 17: Fashion/Shopping): Grammar: Causative Verbs (have/get something done) | Pronunciation: Syllable counting | Prompt: Describe a favorite item of clothing.

Week 18 (Unit 18: Entertainment/Arts): Grammar: Adjective Order | Pronunciation: Expressive intonation for opinions | Prompt: Describe an art exhibition or performance.

Week 19 (Unit 19: Health/Medicine): Grammar: Reported Speech | Pronunciation: Vowel shifting in reported speech | Prompt: Describe a time you were sick and recovered.

Week 20 (Unit 20: The Future): Grammar: Future Continuous (will be V-ing) | Pronunciation: Stressing the '-ing' suffix | Prompt: Describe your life 10 years from now.

📦 BATCH 5: Advanced Phase (Weeks 21–25)
Focus: Revisiting Units 1-5 for IELTS Part 3 (Abstract Logic & Societal Impacts). (2 Complex Sentences per text)

Week 21 (Unit 1: Leisure): Grammar: Hedging (It could be argued...). | Prompt: How has technology changed societal relaxation?

Week 22 (Unit 2: TV & Media): Grammar: Complex Comparisons. | Prompt: What are the dangers of media bias and manipulation?

Week 23 (Unit 3: Food & Diet): Grammar: Third Conditional. | Prompt: Why is there a global epidemic of unhealthy eating?

Week 24 (Unit 4: Transport): Grammar: Future Perfect. | Prompt: How can cities solve gridlock sustainably?

Week 25 (Unit 5: Holidays): Grammar: Non-defining Relative Clauses. | Prompt: What is the cultural impact of commercializing pristine destinations?

📦 BATCH 6: Advanced Phase (Weeks 26–30)
Focus: Revisiting Units 6-10 for IELTS Part 3. (2 Complex Sentences per text)

Week 26 (Unit 6: Education): Grammar: Mixed Conditionals. | Prompt: Should schools prioritize theoretical learning or practical skills?

Week 27 (Unit 7: Careers): Grammar: Passive Reporting Verbs (It is believed...). | Prompt: How is the corporate hierarchy changing?

Week 28 (Unit 8: Work & Money): Grammar: Participle Clauses (Having worked hard...). | Prompt: What are the consequences of a materialistic society?

Week 29 (Unit 9: Sports): Grammar: Inversion (Rarely do we...). | Prompt: Is the high salary of professional athletes justified?

Week 30 (Unit 10: Relationships): Grammar: Cleft Sentences (What I find most interesting is...). | Prompt: How has digital communication affected human empathy?

Section 1. Weekly Grammar Point (5 mins)

So / Such... that
• So + Adj + that: He was so sick that he couldn't walk.
• Such + (Adj) Noun + that: It was such a nice day that we went out.
So/Such... that：如此...以至于。So 修饰形容词/副词；Such 修饰名词。

Section 2. Weekly Pronunciation Point (3 mins)

Emphasis on So/Such
Stress 'so' or 'such' to emphasize the degree.
• It was SO hot that...
• It was SUCH a good film that...
强调 So/Such：重读这两个词以强调程度。

Recycled Words: (Included in list above)
Idiom 1: Back on one's feet (恢复健康 / 东山再起)
Explanation: (生病或遇到困难后) 完全康复或恢复正常。
Example: After spending a week in the hospital with the flu, I am finally back on my feet.
Idiom 2: Take a toll on (造成损害 / 付出代价)
Explanation: 对某人或某物造成严重的损害或负面影响。
Example: Working late every single night will eventually take a toll on your health.

  2. Does a sedentary lifestyle evoke fear of chronic illness? (Recycled)
  3. Why are back problems so prevalent today?

Section 5. Shadowing text adapted from the Cambridge IGCSE ESL Coursebook (3 mins)

It is alarming that we are living longer but sicker. Actually, a sedentary lifestyle is so prevalent that everyone sits all day. This habit takes a toll on our bodies. Our health can deteriorate so fast that we develop chronic pain. Doctors say we need a holistic approach. We need to fix the mind and body. It is such a profound problem that medicine alone isn't enough. Even thinking about it can evoke worry. However, with exercise, you can get back on your feet. We aim for longevity with quality.

Section 7. Model Answer (5 mins)

It is clear that longevity is increasing. Actually, modern medicine is amazing. However, chronic diseases are prevalent. Some elderly people are so lonely that their mental health deteriorates. Also, a sedentary life takes a toll on mobility. This creates such a heavy burden on families that we need a holistic care system. Seeing them suffer evokes profound sadness. We must help them get back on their feet emotionally. To sum up, care is essential.

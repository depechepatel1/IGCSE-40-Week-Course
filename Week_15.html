Student Handout

Section 1. Weekly Grammar Point (5 mins)

Question Tags
• Positive Statement -> Negative Tag: You are happy, aren't you?
• Negative Statement -> Positive Tag: He isn't coming, is he?
反意疑问句：前肯后否，前否后肯。用于确认信息或征求同意。

Section 2. Weekly Pronunciation Point (3 mins)

Intonation in Question Tags
• Falling Intonation (↘): You are sure/expecting agreement. (It's nice, isn't it?↘)
• Rising Intonation (↗): You are not sure/asking a real question. (She is coming, isn't she?↗)
反意疑问句语调：降调表示确定/寻求认同；升调表示不确定/真提问。

Idiom 1: Do as the Romans do (入乡随俗)
Explanation: 到了一个新地方，就要遵守那里的风俗习惯。
Example: I ate with my hands while traveling in India, because when in Rome, you should do as the Romans do.
Idiom 2: Broaden one's horizons (开阔眼界)
Explanation: 增长见识，扩大视野。
Example: Traveling to different countries is a great way to broaden your horizons.

Section 5. Shadowing text adapted from the Cambridge IGCSE ESL Coursebook (3 mins)

To be honest, China has a rich heritage. Our country is very diverse, isn't it? Actually, every region has its own customs. It is important to show respect to elders, which is a key part of our etiquette. However, some traditions are changing. For example, young people often send virtual red envelopes now. That is quite innovative, isn't it? We should try to broaden our horizons by learning about other cultures, but we must preserve our own traditions too. Remember, when in Rome, do as the Romans do.

Section 7. Model Answer (5 mins)

Well, I love the Spring Festival. Actually, it is the most important tradition in China. To be honest, the food is amazing, isn't it? My family always follows the custom of making dumplings together, which is part of our cultural heritage. Also, we have to follow strict etiquette when greeting elders. Although modern life is diverse and fast, we still respect these old ways. Sometimes, we use innovative apps to send virtual greetings. To sum up, this festival helps me broaden my horizons about my own culture.

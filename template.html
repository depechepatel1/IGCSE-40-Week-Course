⏱️ Lesson Plan Table (80 Minutes)

Timing Teacher Actions (QTS Steps) Student Actions & Notes
0-10m Warm-Up & Baseline: Introduce WALT/WILF. Direct students to Sec 4. Circulate the room to listen for diagnostic baseline fluency. Do not interrupt for error correction yet. Pair Work: Students discuss Warm-Up Qs, attempting to retrieve basic middle school vocabulary and get comfortable speaking English.
10-25m Input & Pronunciation: Read Sec 5 aloud emphasizing natural rhythm. Explicitly teach Sec 1 (Grammar) and drill Sec 2 (Pronunciation). Explain the color codes. Active Listening: Annotate handouts. Choral repetition of the phonetic linking drill ("have‿a blast"). Review the Vocab table.
25-45m Drill (Brainstorming): Reveal Sec 6 (Circuit Prompt). Model how to populate the Spidergram using Sec 3 vocabulary. Check written work in Sec 8. Mind-Mapping: Students build their individual spidergrams and draft their 1 single complex sentence in the writing space.
45-70m Speaking Circuit (4-3-2): Explain AfL Listener Task (Sec 9). Manage the timer strictly (4 mins, rotate, 3 mins, rotate, 2 mins). Note errors on a clipboard. Fluency Practice: Speaker speaks continuously based on spidergram. Listener completes tally and asks Stretch question if time permits.
70-80m Exit Ticket & HW: Write 2-3 common errors anonymously on the board. Supervise Sec 11. Explain Pre (Sec 12) and Post (Sec 13) App tasks. Consolidation: Correct board errors in their Exit Ticket space, pack belongings, and log into the App to note HW requirements.

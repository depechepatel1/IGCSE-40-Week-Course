Section 1. Weekly Grammar Point (5 mins)

Future in the Past
• Structure: Was/Were going to + Verb.
• Use: Plans in the past that usually didn't happen.
• I was going to buy it, but it was too expensive.
过去将来时：Was/Were going to...。表示过去原本打算做某事（通常后来没做成）。

Section 2. Weekly Pronunciation Point (3 mins)

Intonation of Contrast
Use a falling intonation on the first part (plan), and a rising intonation on 'but' (contrast).
• I was going to GO (↘), BUT (↗)...
转折的语调：前半句（计划）用降调，but（转折）用升调。

Idiom 1: Light years ahead (遥遥领先 / 望尘莫及)
Explanation: 在发展或技术上遥遥领先于其他人。
Example: The new AI technology is light years ahead of what we had ten years ago.
Idiom 2: Reinvent the wheel (多此一举 / 重新发明轮子)
Explanation: 白费力气去重新发明已经存在且有效的东西。
Example: The software already works perfectly, so there is no need to reinvent the wheel.

Section 5. Shadowing text adapted from the Cambridge IGCSE ESL Coursebook (3 mins)

It is fascinating that tech evolves so fast. Last year, our school was going to buy new computers. However, they became obsolete very quickly. They were going to automate the library system, but the algorithm was too sophisticated. Now, AI is a new paradigm. Some people worry it will cause irreversible job losses. We were going to mitigate this by training staff, but the change is happening too fast. Actually, this tech is light years ahead of old tools. We shouldn't reinvent the wheel; we should use it. Technology is now indispensable.

Section 7. Model Answer (5 mins)

It is interesting that plans often fail due to tech speed. Actually, my company was going to launch a simple app. To be honest, we realized the code was obsolete. We were going to automate customer service, but we needed a more sophisticated algorithm. This shift represents a new paradigm. Although the delay was frustrating, we managed to mitigate the loss and build something indispensable. Our new AI is light years ahead. To sum up, adaptation is key to avoid irreversible failure.

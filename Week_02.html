WALT (We Are Learning To): Discuss memorable TV shows and movies using comparatives and superlatives. Evaluate content effectively using target vocabulary.

WILF (What I'm Looking For): 1. Exactly ONE yellow complex sentence.

2. At least TWO blue sentence starters.

3. Accurate use of the bold weekly vocabulary.

Provide sentence frames: "My favorite show is ____ because it is more..." and "I think documentaries are the most..." Allow them to rely on their Chinese translation table.

🚀 Stretch (Band 6.0+ Target)

Demand exact execution of the complex sentence without reading. Direct fast-finishers to the Part 3 Differentiation Stretch question (Section 10) to force spontaneous comparing and evaluating.

Section 1. Weekly Grammar Point (5 mins)

Comparatives & Superlatives
• Comparatives: Compare two things (e.g., News is more informative than cartoons. / This show is funnier than that one.).
比较级：比较两件事物 (例如: News is more informative than cartoons. / This show is funnier than that one.)
• Superlatives: Compare three or more things (e.g., It is the most entertaining show on TV. / He is the best actor.).
最高级：比较三件或更多事物 (例如: It is the most entertaining show on TV. / He is the best actor.)

Section 2. Weekly Pronunciation Point (3 mins)

Word Stress in Multi-syllable Adjectives
English words have one stressed syllable that is longer, louder, and higher in pitch. If you stress the wrong syllable, it can be hard to understand.
英语单词有一个重读音节，发音更长、更响亮、音调更高。如果你重读错误的音节，可能会很难理解。
• Say: In-FOR-ma-tive (Stress the 2nd syllable)
• Say: En-ter-TAIN-ing (Stress the 3rd syllable)
• Say: Ad-DIC-tive (Stress the 2nd syllable)

Idiom 1: Glued to the screen (目不转睛 / 紧盯屏幕)
Explanation: 形容人全神贯注地盯着电视或手机屏幕看。
Example: My little brother is always glued to the screen when his favorite cartoon is on.
Idiom 2: Make headlines (登上头条 / 轰动一时)
Explanation: 成为新闻的重要报道，引起广泛关注。
Example: The release of the new smartphone made headlines yesterday.

Section 4. Warm-Up Questions (10 mins)

  1. (Section 5 Question) What show on TV do you find most fascinating and why?
  2. Do you usually participate in any sports to unwind after school?
  3. Are you more of a couch potato or an active enthusiast on weekends?

Section 5. Shadowing text adapted from the Cambridge IGCSE ESL Coursebook (3 mins)

Honestly speaking, I spend too much time on my phone. These days, short videos are very addictive. I subscribe to many funny channels because they are entertaining. However, my parents think I should watch the news broadcast. They say it is more informative than cartoons. Sometimes, I agree with them. Actually, watching a good documentary is a great way to unwind. Although the commercials are annoying, the show itself is fascinating. In conclusion, I try not to be glued to the screen all day.

Section 7. Model Answer (5 mins)

Well, the most memorable show for me is 'Planet Earth'. Basically, it is a nature documentary that is broadcast on the BBC. To be honest, it is much more fascinating than a normal movie. I subscribe to their channel so I never miss an episode. The images are so beautiful that I am always glued to the screen. In fact, it often makes headlines around the world. Even though it is educational, it is also highly entertaining and addictive. All in all, it is the most informative show I have ever seen.

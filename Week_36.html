Section 1. Weekly Grammar Point (5 mins)

Concessive Clauses
• Much as: Much as I like him, I can't agree. (Although I like him...).
• However + Adj/Adv: However hard I try, I fail.
• In spite of: In spite of the rain, we went.
让步状语从句：Much as (尽管), However + Adj/Adv (无论多么...), In spite of (尽管)。

Section 2. Weekly Pronunciation Point (3 mins)

Intonation in Concessive Clauses
Use a rise-fall tone on the concessive clause to show contrast.
• Much as I LIKE it (↘↗), I can't buy it (↘).
让步从句的语调：在从句中使用升降调，以表示转折。

Recycled Words: (Included in list above)
Idiom 1: Concrete jungle (钢筋水泥丛林)
Explanation: 钢筋水泥的丛林，指人口密集且缺乏绿色的现代大都市。
Example: Sometimes living in this concrete jungle makes me miss the green trees of my hometown.
Idiom 2: Rat race (激烈竞争 / 劳碌奔波)
Explanation: 激烈且永无休止的竞争，通常指城市中的职场压力。
Example: He decided to leave his corporate job to escape the daily rat race.

Section 4. Warm-Up Questions (10 mins)

  1. (Section 5 Question) Does rapid urbanization create better amenitys or social alienation?
  2. Can we preserve nature in a sprawling city? (Recycled)
  3. Do you prefer to be anonymous in a big city?

Section 5. Shadowing text adapted from the Cambridge IGCSE ESL Coursebook (3 mins)

It is true that cities offer jobs. However, rapid urbanization causes problems. Much as I love the convenience of every modern amenity, the noise is stressful. Also, cities can feel like a concrete jungle. People often feel anonymous and lonely. However hard we try to build communities, social alienation is increasing. Furthermore, urban sprawl destroys nature. It tends to displace local farmers. Some developers homogenize neighborhoods, making them look the same. We must try to preserve green spaces. Escaping the rat race is becoming a priority for many.

Section 7. Model Answer (5 mins)

It is widely accepted that cities drive the economy. Actually, urbanization brings better hospitals and schools. To be honest, much as I enjoy the amenitys, I hate the pollution. In spite of living among millions, it is easy to feel anonymous and suffer from alienation. Also, the sprawl of housing threatens to displace wildlife. However fast the city grows, we must preserve our parks. Otherwise, we just live in a concrete jungle. Modern life tends to homogenize our experiences. To sum up, we need balance to avoid the rat race.
